Lane Segmentation

To obtain the lane segmentation, an encoder-decoder network architecture was used. This model was based on the SegNet architecture. Segnet first performs convolution operations on the image, in the encoder stage of the network, to form a compressed representation of the images features. In the decoder network a mixture of up-sampling and transposed convolutions to transform the compressed features back into an image which allows the model to perform pixel wise classification on the image.

To train the network approximately 20,000 images of segmented lanes were collected, giving 40,000 training samples when flipped horizontally. On long roads, the network performs fairly well, however in an urban environment it doesn't do as well.
